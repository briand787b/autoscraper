Ford F-150 Supercrew Cab Guide

Get the best online car shopping experience with expert reviews of the Ford F-150 Supercrew Cab. Read more to learn about the most important features and important factors to consider when researching the Ford F-150 Supercrew Cab.

FORD F150 SUPERCREW CABOverview

Ford has a tradition of making high-end pickup trucks that are fine-tuned for performance, comfort and off-road capability. The Ford F-150 SuperCrew Cab is one of the pickup trucks in the Ford Motor Company's lineup. Driving a used F-150 SuperCrew Cab gives you access to some of the latest accessories, features and capabilities built into the truck.

NOTEWORTHY FEATURES

Wide Range of Cab Options

The Ford F-150 SuperCrew Cab has an appealing and upscale range of trim levels to choose from. The King Ranch, Platinum and Limited trims are some of the best-in-market models that you can select for extended features, capability and performance. In almost all trim levels, this pickup truck offers distinctive interior luxury features and options, including heated leather in all seats plus premium entertainment systems.

Extended Utility Functions

Being a pickup truck, the Ford F-150 SuperCrew Cab is designed to withstand and accommodate a wide range of utility functions. In addition to its spacious interior and ample cargo bed, this truck comes with small-item-storage compartments, including cup holders, door pockets and glove boxes. With the F-150 SuperCrew Cab's towing capacity of up to 13,200 pounds, you can extend your carrying capacity beyond the rear cargo compartment.

Tech Accessories

The Ford F-150 SuperCrew Cab is designed with an array of technological features and accessories on the interior. The built-in GPS navigation system lets you find your way both in city environments and while off-roading. The accompanying Apple CarPlay and Android Auto smartphone integration feature allow you to pair your mobile phones with the vehicle to answer calls, read messages and charge your devices.

ADDITIONAL CONSIDERATIONS

Less Stable Ride When Empty

The Ford F-150 SuperCrew pickup cab is designed to optimize its capability only when carrying or hauling cargo. Driving the vehicle when the cargo bed is empty significantly compromises the vehicle's stability. This is mainly the case when driving on the highway, where you may feel that the truck lacks sufficient road grip when moving at moderate to high speeds.

Limited Fuel Efficiency

The Ford F-150 SuperCrew Cab's typical engine is a 5-liter V8 that may have fuel ratings of up to 17 mpg in the city and 23 mpg on the highway. With this fuel consumption rating highly likely to increase when you have payloads, it is clear that the F-150 SuperCrew Cab may require you to keep it fueled daily.

High Ride Challenge

The Ford F-150 SuperCrew Cab is designed for the tough off-loading conditions where high ground clearance is a significant advantage. This implies that boarding the vehicle may have you assume some inconvenient postures, such as jumping or even being assisted to board, especially for children. Some trims, such as the Lariat, solve this problem with running boards, while others have this feature as an optional accessory.
